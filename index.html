About

Leadership that turns equity into practice

I am a public health leader, educator, and applied researcher with more than 20 years of experience advancing health equity across nonprofit, academic, government, and community settings. My work sits where strategy meets execution: directing complex, multi-million-dollar program portfolios, building and developing teams, and translating research into tools that practitioners actually use.

As Associate Director of the Center for Health Equity Practice at the Michigan Public Health Institute, I co-developed a nationally recognized theory of change for advancing equity-driven systems change in governmental public health — work carried out in partnership with the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation, the CDC Foundation, and ASTHO, and now in use by state health agencies across the country.

Across every role, my throughline is the same: rigorous program design and evaluation, sustained community partnership, and capacity building that leaves organizations stronger than I found them.

For nearly a decade, I designed and co-led immersive, community-based field research programs in Ghana, Brazil, and Costa Rica — training more than 135 students in ethical global health practice, applied research methods, and genuine community partnership.
